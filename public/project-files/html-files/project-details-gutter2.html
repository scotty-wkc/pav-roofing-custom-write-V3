Project Overview

Complete rainwater harvesting system installation for an environmentally conscious family on the Central Coast. The project focused on maximizing water collection efficiency while integrating seamlessly with the existing home design.

The Challenge

The homeowners contacted us with a clear vision: they wanted to reduce their reliance on mains water supply and create a sustainable rainwater collection system for their garden irrigation. However, several challenges needed to be addressed:

  • Existing gutters were undersized for the roof catchment area, causing overflow during heavy rainfall
  • Poor water quality due to inadequate leaf and debris filtering
  • Inefficient downpipe positioning limiting water collection potential
  • Need for a system that could handle Central Coast's variable rainfall patterns
  • Integration requirements with existing landscaping and a 5,000-liter underground storage tank

The homeowners also emphasized the importance of a low-maintenance solution that would provide clean water suitable for garden use while maintaining the home's aesthetic appeal.

Our Solution

After conducting a detailed site assessment and water flow analysis, we designed a comprehensive rainwater harvesting solution:

  1. Gutter Upgrade: Replaced existing gutters with larger capacity colorbond gutters designed to handle the full roof catchment area without overflow.
  2. Strategic Downpipe Placement: Repositioned and added downpipes to optimize water collection and direct flow efficiently to the storage system.
  3. First Flush Diverter: Installed an automatic first flush diverter to ensure the initial roof runoff (containing most debris) is diverted away from the storage tank.
  4. Premium Leaf Guards: Integrated high-quality leaf guards and gutter screens to minimize maintenance and prevent blockages.
  5. Underground Connection: Created a seamless connection to the existing underground storage tank with proper overflow management.
  6. Distribution System: Set up a gravity-fed distribution network for efficient garden irrigation.

Our design maximized the 180 square meter roof catchment area, potentially collecting over 90,000 liters annually based on local rainfall data.

The Execution

The project was completed efficiently over three days:

Day 1: Assessment and Preparation

We began with a final site survey and water flow calculations. The existing gutter system was carefully removed, and we prepared mounting points for the new larger gutters. Underground tank connections were inspected and prepared for integration.

Day 2: Primary Installation

The new colorbond gutter system was installed with precise falls to ensure optimal water flow. Downpipes were positioned strategically, and the first flush diverter system was connected. All connections were tested for proper water flow and sealed against leaks.

Day 3: Completion and Testing

Leaf guards were fitted across the entire gutter system. The underground tank connections were completed with overflow protection. We conducted comprehensive testing using simulated rainfall to ensure the system operated at full efficiency. The homeowners were provided with a complete maintenance guide.

Throughout the project, we coordinated closely with the homeowners' landscaper to ensure minimal disruption to existing garden beds and pathways.

The Results

The completed rainwater harvesting system exceeded expectations:

  • Increased water collection efficiency by 300% compared to the previous system
  • Clean, filtered water suitable for all garden irrigation needs
  • Elimination of gutter overflow issues during heavy rainfall
  • Reduced annual water bills by an estimated $800-$1,200
  • Low-maintenance system requiring only quarterly inspections
  • Enhanced property sustainability credentials and value

The system performed exceptionally well during the first major rainfall event post-installation, collecting over 1,500 liters in a single day. The homeowners were particularly impressed with the water quality and the system's automatic operation.
