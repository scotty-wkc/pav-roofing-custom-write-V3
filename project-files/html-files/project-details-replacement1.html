Project Overview

The Emergency Situation

During a severe storm in February 2023, this Parramatta home suffered extensive roof damage that required immediate attention:

  • Large tree branch had punctured through the roof into the living area
  • Multiple broken and displaced tiles across 40% of the roof surface
  • Damaged guttering and downpipes causing water overflow
  • Compromised roof structure with several cracked battens and rafters
  • Active water leaks threatening interior furnishings and electrical systems

The homeowners contacted us in the early hours following the storm, requiring immediate emergency response to prevent further damage while coordinating with their insurance company for a permanent solution.

Our Emergency Response & Solution

We implemented a comprehensive two-phase approach to address both immediate and long-term needs:

Phase 1: Emergency Stabilization (Day 1)

  1. Immediate Response: Emergency call-out within 2 hours to assess damage and secure the property with professional-grade tarpaulins.
  2. Safety Assessment: Thorough structural evaluation to identify immediate hazards and required repairs.
  3. Insurance Documentation: Comprehensive damage photography and documentation to support the insurance claim process.

Phase 2: Complete Replacement (Days 2-6)

  1. Structural Repairs: Replacement of damaged rafters, battens, and roof decking to ensure structural integrity.
  2. Upgraded Materials: Installation of impact-resistant tiles and enhanced underlayment for superior storm protection.
  3. Improved Systems: Upgraded guttering, enhanced insulation, and improved ventilation to exceed original specifications.

Project Execution

Emergency Response (Day 1)

Upon receiving the emergency call at 2:30 AM, our team was on-site by 4:45 AM with emergency equipment. We immediately secured the damaged areas with heavy-duty tarpaulins and established temporary water diversion to protect the interior.

Assessment and Planning (Day 2)

Once daylight allowed for proper assessment, our structural engineer evaluated the damage extent. We worked directly with the insurance adjuster to document all damage and secure approval for the comprehensive replacement approach.

Structural Repairs (Days 3-4)

The damaged structural elements were carefully removed and replaced with upgraded materials. Special attention was given to reinforcing areas that showed vulnerability to future storm damage.

Roofing Installation (Days 5-6)

The new impact-resistant tile system was installed with enhanced fastening techniques designed to withstand severe weather conditions. All flashing and waterproofing exceeded standard requirements.

Outstanding Results

The emergency response and complete roof replacement delivered exceptional outcomes:

  • Zero additional water damage during the 6-day project timeline
  • Roof now rated for Category 3 cyclone conditions with impact-resistant tiles
  • 30% improvement in thermal efficiency through upgraded insulation
  • Enhanced property value exceeding pre-storm condition by 15%
  • Comprehensive 25-year warranty on all materials and workmanship
