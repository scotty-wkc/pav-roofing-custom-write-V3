When to Replace Your Roof

Recognizing these common signs can help you determine if it's time for a roof replacement.

Age

Most asphalt shingle roofs last 20-25 years, while metal roofs can last 40-70 years. If your roof is approaching the end of its expected lifespan, it's time to consider replacement.

Leaks

Water stains on ceilings or walls, or active leaks during rainfall, indicate significant roof damage that may require a full replacement.

Missing Shingles

Extensive missing, cracked, or curling shingles throughout your roof suggest system-wide deterioration and the need for replacement rather than repairs.

Granule Loss

Finding large amounts of granules in your gutters indicates shingle deterioration, reducing your roof's ability to protect your home from UV rays and water.

Sagging Roof

A sagging roof deck suggests structural issues that need immediate attention, often requiring a complete roof replacement.

Energy Efficiency

If your energy bills are climbing, your roof may be losing its insulating properties, making a replacement with modern, energy-efficient materials a cost-effective solution.

Our Roof Replacement Process

At Pav's Roofing Solutions, we follow a comprehensive process to ensure your roof replacement project is completed efficiently, with minimal disruption and maximum quality.

1

Initial Consultation & Inspection

We begin with a thorough inspection of your current roof, identifying damage, structural issues, and determining if replacement is necessary. We discuss your goals, preferences, and budget.

2

Detailed Proposal

Based on our inspection, we provide a comprehensive proposal including recommended materials, project timeline, and transparent pricing with no hidden costs.

3

Material Selection

We help you select the right roofing materials for your property, considering factors like durability, aesthetics, energy efficiency, and budget. We offer various options including asphalt shingles, metal roofing, tile, and slate.

4

Project Preparation

Before work begins, we obtain any necessary permits, schedule delivery of materials, and prepare your property to minimize disruption and protect landscaping, windows, and exterior features.

5

Roof Removal

We carefully remove and dispose of your old roofing materials, inspecting the underlying deck for any damage that needs to be addressed before installation begins.

6

Installation

Our experienced team installs your new roof using industry best practices and manufacturer specifications to ensure optimal performance and warranty protection.

7

Final Inspection & Cleanup

We conduct a thorough inspection of the completed work, ensure proper cleanup of all debris, and walk you through the final result, addressing any questions or concerns.

8

Warranty & Follow-up

We provide detailed warranty information for both materials and workmanship, and schedule a follow-up inspection to ensure your complete satisfaction.
